Chester Bennington

March 20, 1976 - July 20, 2017

    Chester Bennington's Career Timeline:

  • 1993 - Became a part of his first band, Sean Dowdell and His Friends?.
  • 2000 - Chester Bennington joined Linkin Park and released Hybrid Theory
  • 2003 - Chester gave the vocals for the Linkin Park's second album Meteora
  • 2005 - Founded his own rock band named the Dead by Sunrise
  • 2006 - Chester played the role of a pharmacy customer, adrenaline junkie in the American action film, Crank. The film was directed by Mark Neveldine and Brian Taylor.
  • 2007 - Chester's third album Minutes to Midnight was released. With over 600,000 copies in its first week and gaining the top spot on the Billboard Charts, it became one of the most successful albums.
  • 2009 - As a part of the band Dead by Sunrise, he came out with the band’s debut album, Out of Ashes. The album was moderately successful.
  • 2010 - Chester gave the vocals for the Linkin Park's fourth album, A Thousand Suns, which was a muti-concept album that highlighted the concept of nuclear warfare. The album received positive reviews.
  • 2012 - Chester featured as the lead singer for the Linkin Park's album, Living Things, which was one among the band’s bestselling albums
  • 2014 - Chester featured as the lead singer for the Linkin Park's 6th album, The Hunting Party.
  • 2017 - Chester featured as the lead singer for the Linkin Park's 7th album, One More Light, which was one among the band’s bestselling and last albums
